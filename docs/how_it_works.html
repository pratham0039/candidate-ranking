2 · What the embeddings actually do

An embedding turns a paragraph of text into a list of 384 numbers that capture its meaning. Texts about the same idea get similar numbers, even when they share no words. We embed two things with the same model (MiniLM, runs on CPU):

Each candidate's career text (precomputed once, 13 min)
"Built a RAG-based ranking pipeline… BM25 + dense retrieval (BGE embeddings, FAISS HNSW)…"
→ [0.031, −0.118, 0.054, … 0.077]
⟶
The job description (one vector)
"Production experience with embeddings-based retrieval… vector databases… evaluation frameworks for ranking…"
→ [0.044, −0.092, 0.061, … 0.083]
At ranking time we just measure the angle between each candidate's vector and the JD vector (cosine similarity) — one multiplication per candidate, which is why ranking takes 14 s after the one-time precompute.

Why bother? Because the best candidates don't always use the JD's words. One top profile wrote "systems that connect users with relevant information at scale" — zero buzzwords, but its embedding lands close to the JD's. Keyword matching misses this person; the embedding doesn't.

3 · The score: one number per candidate

score = skill_fit × yoe1.26 × location0.22 × availability0.33 × penalties0.83

skill_fit = (0.13·evidence + 0.76·tfidf_sim + 0.25·embed_sim) / 1.14
evidenceHow many JD must-have competencies the career text demonstrates (embeddings work, vector infra, ranking systems, evaluation, Python).
tfidf_simWord-level similarity with the JD, weighted toward rare, telling terms (FAISS, NDCG…).
embed_simThe meaning-level similarity from section 2.
yoeTrapezoid: 1.0 at 6–8 yrs, ramps at 5/9, decays outside.
locationPune/Noida 1.0 · welcome metros 0.9 · rest of India 0.55–0.75 · abroad ≤0.35.
availabilityLogin recency (halves every 45 idle days) + reply rate + open-to-work + notice period.
penaltiesDampeners for what the JD rejects: services-only careers, title-chasing, CV/speech-only.

Factors multiply, so no single strength can paper over a broken dimension — a perfect-on-paper ghost who never replies still sinks. The exponents weren't guessed: 4,000 random combinations were tested against our offline benchmark and the best kept.
