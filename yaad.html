The problem we want to solve

Life in India arrives as a forward.

Exam timetables, fee circulars, water-cut notices, placement drives, they reach you as blurry JPEGs and rambling voice notes, buried inside a dozen WhatsApp groups.

  • Who faces it: a student is in 15+ college groups; a parent in school and society groups; an office-goer in team and PG groups. Everyone.
  • The failure mode: the information was delivered, but never captured. It scrolls away, and the deadline arrives unannounced.
  • Why it matters: a missed placement registration, a fee late-fine, an exam clash discovered too late. Small misses, real consequences.

Calendar apps assume people will type structured events. Nobody does. The information already lives in WhatsApp, the capture should too.

03
Idea overview

My memory. Our memory. My radar.

FikarNot is one WhatsApp contact. The capture gesture is the forward button, no app, no forms. On top of it sit three layers of intelligence, each one harder to copy than the last.

Layer 1 · Personal

A memory that notices

Forward anything and it remembers, reminds, and connects facts across weeks: your train ticket vs. your exam vs. your fee deadline. Reminder apps remember. FikarNot notices.

Layer 2 · Collective, the moat

One forwards. All covered.

When any classmate forwards a notice, the cohort's shared memory (opt-in) gets it, even the student who was asleep. And consensus across copies auto-fixes blurry reads.

Layer 3 · Career radar, only Redrob

Notices that know your future

A placement notice comes back eligibility-checked against Redrob's job data: "CGPA cutoff 7.5, you qualify (yours: 8.1). Want to prep the interview on Redrob?"

Why only Redrob can ship this

Layer 3 is the un-copyable part: eligibility checks, resume matching, and interview prep run on Redrob's hiring data: profiles, postings, and placement signals no general assistant or wrapper bot has. Every TPO notice in India becomes a Redrob job-funnel entry, arriving via WhatsApp.

Fit with the Redrob ecosystem

FikarNot turns Redrob from a tool users visit occasionally into a companion that messages them every morning, running affordably at Bharat scale on Redrob's stated low-cost LLM stack, in the 30+ languages the platform publicly supports. The understanding layer is model-agnostic, Redrob's data, not any single model, is the moat.

08
AI-powered experience

Where the intelligence lives

Understand

Messy input → structured memory

OCR on notice photos, speech recognition on voice notes, intent + entity extraction (event, date, person, action), across 30+ Indian languages and code-mixed Hinglish: exactly the workload a Redrob-scale multilingual stack is positioned to own. Low-confidence reads trigger a confirm, never a silent save.

Remember

A memory graph, in two scopes

Facts stored with relationships (dates, people, places) in two scopes: personal (private by default) and cohort (opt-in). Cohorts form from the notice itself: identical document fingerprints across opted-in users make one cohort, no group access needed, and copies cross-correct by consensus.

Notice

Conflicts checked at two moments

Every new memory is checked against the graph the instant it's written, and the whole graph is re-checked each morning. Catch clashes at upload; catch slow-burn ones at dawn.

Restraint

Silence as a feature

No daily "have a great day!" spam. FikarNot speaks only on signal, a real deadline, a real conflict. A muted bot is a dead bot. And three things FikarNot will never do: message the group, sell your data, chitchat.

Why AI is essential: no rules engine can read a crumpled Marathi notice photographed at an angle, hear "agle mangalvaar" in a voice note and resolve it to a date, or judge that a train ticket and an exam timetable are talking about the same Tuesday.

Notes for the technically curious

  • Two-checkpoint conflict engine: new facts are matched against the graph on date / person / place overlap at write time; the full day-graph is re-evaluated every morning.
  • Cohort formation without group access: the notice is the key. A perceptual image hash plus extracted entities fingerprints each document; identical fingerprints across opted-in users form the cohort and power consensus reads.
  • Why a fact graph + vectors, not vectors alone: "train on the 18th clashes with exam on the 19th" is a relationship query, embeddings retrieve text, graphs catch collisions.
  • WhatsApp 24-hour rule, handled: replies are free-form inside the session window; proactive reminders use pre-approved template messages, compliant by design.
  • Honest unit economics: WhatsApp utility templates cost ₹0.115–0.145 each in India (Meta rate card, Jan 2026), and are free inside an open 24-hour service window; digest batching caps usage at ~30 templates/user/month. All-in (templates + LLM inference) ≈ a few rupees per user per month, paid back if even ~1% of users convert into a Redrob job-funnel session.

Appendix B · Technical deep-dive: memory graph & cohort consensus
1 · The graph = typed rows 
documents(id, fingerprint, media_ref,
          extracted_json, scope, cohort_id)
facts(id, doc_id, kind, title,
      start_ts, end_ts, location,
      confidence, remind_at, scope)

Postgres + pgvector. Nodes are fact rows; edges are foreign keys (fact → document → cohort). No graph database needed at this scale; that's a feature, not a compromise.

2 · Extraction with a confidence gate 
{"doc_type": "exam_timetable",
 "events": [{"title": "DBMS exam",
   "date": "2026-06-19", "time": "09:00",
   "location": "Block A",
   "confidence": 0.93}]}

One LLM call per message, strict JSON schema. Confidence ≥ 0.85 saves silently; anything below triggers the confirm message. That single threshold is the trust UX.

3 · Conflict = a range query, not ML 
SELECT * FROM facts
WHERE user_id = :u AND status = 'active'
  AND tstzrange(start_ts, end_ts) &&
      tstzrange(:new_start, :new_end)
  AND kind <> :new_kind;

Run at two checkpoints: synchronously on every write (the instant clash reply) and in the 7 AM cron over the next 72 hours (the digest). Train 18–22 ∩ exam 19 returns one row; that row is the warning.

4 · Cohorts & consensus, from the document itself

Fingerprint = image pHash + hash of extracted entities; Hamming distance ≤ 8 means the same notice. Opted-in users holding matching fingerprints form one cohort. No group access needed.

DBMS date vote:
  19 Jun: 11 copies, Σ conf 9.8  ✓
  18 Jun:  3 copies, Σ conf 1.9
→ canonical = 19 Jun, provenance kept

Close votes (under ~70/30) fall back to a confirm. "Yeh bhool jao" deletes a user's facts and provenance; the cohort canonical survives if others remain.

Appendix D · The hard questions, answered before you ask

"Which Redrob models does this actually run on?"

The understanding layer is deliberately model-agnostic: standard OCR/ASR components, with Redrob's LLM (the company's publicly stated 30+ language, low-cost stack) for extraction. The honest moat was never a model. It's Redrob's hiring data (profiles, postings, placement signals) that powers Layer 3, and no model swap replicates that.

"If the notice is buried in 371 unread, how does the user know to forward it?"

They don't have to, that's why cohort memory is core, not a feature. Any one of 60 classmates forwarding covers everyone (one CR is enough to seed a class). Roadmap closes the loop fully: group-native mode, where FikarNot sits in the group and reads notices at the source.

"What if FikarNot misreads a date and I miss my exam?"

Three layers of defense: low-confidence reads trigger a confirm, never a silent save; cohort consensus cross-checks every copy of the same notice; and every reminder cites its source, so the original is one tap away. Wrong-read rate is a tracked core metric, and FikarNot is an assistive layer over the notice, never a replacement for it.

"The WhatsApp API doesn't tell you which group a forward came from. Your mockups show it."

Correct: the API exposes only a forwarded flag, and we never need group access. Identical forwards are matched by document fingerprint (perceptual hash plus extracted entities), which both forms the cohort and supplies context. Documents also self-identify (letterheads, dept names), and group-native mode adds full context where groups adopt it.

"Meta AI is adding persistent memory to WhatsApp. Then what?"

Memory is the feature Meta can copy; our moats aren't memory. They're the India-specific workload (blurry vernacular notice photos as the core competency, not an edge case), cohort consensus (a network, not a feature), and Redrob's hiring data (Layer 3). And memory itself compounds, a year of your life in FikarNot is a switching cost no fresh assistant resets.

"How are the memory graph and shared memory actually implemented?"

Facts are nodes (event, date, person, place) with typed edges; conflict detection is a constraint query on date/person/place overlap, at write time and each dawn. Cohort memory is a shared namespace keyed to group identity, with per-fact provenance: who forwarded, how many copies, consensus score. Personal scope is a separate namespace, never merged.
